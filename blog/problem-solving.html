Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

  1. Pseudocode
    I am still not entirely used to writing Pseudocode yet however I am getting better at it and found it very useful during the last 2 Katas.
  2. Trying something
    This is something that I usually resort to first as my first instinct is always oh well I haven't got it correct yet. However this shouldn't always be my go to response.
  3. Rubber ducky method
    I have not really used this yet however since reading good things about it maybe it is something I should try to incorporate to my problem solving.
  4. Reading error messages
    Generally speaking I am pretty comfortable with these and they have been a great help for time saving and locating where the mistake might be faster.
  5. Console.logging
    Usually not my first response however this is something I am coming to use more and more especially having seen Joseph use it quite commonly in his demo videos to check things.
  6. Googling
    Being a few weeks in to the course I have really found websites and resources that I know I can rely on to quickly access correcy and helpful information.
  7. Asking my peers for help
    I have been fortunate to get to speak to a couple of other Christchurch students who have been very kind and very helpful towards me when I have gotten stuck. Especially in previous weeks they helped alot.
  8. Asking coaches for help
    Often I feel like my issue isn't "pressing enough" or worth the coaches time which I think is the incorrect mindset and I really should be less afraid to ask for help when I've gotten stuck.

    The past 2 weeks or so I have tried to make a lot of effort towards given detailed relfections as I think that reflecting on the week in detail better allows me to see where I could have done better during the week.
